Five tabs, each tied to a scoring area:
Orbit Decay — plots observed altitude from each TLE epoch + your SGP4-propagated future. The three race altitude bands (≈500km Hello World, active race zone, ≈300km finish) are drawn as reference lines. The "Est. reentry" metric uses your observed decay rate to project when the ChipSat hits ~120km, which is the core race timeline.
Propagated Track — runs SGP4 from whichever TLE you've selected in the sidebar, stepping through your chosen time window. This is what you use to schedule ground station contacts before a pass occurs.
Model Residuals — this is the core validation loop. For each consecutive TLE pair, it propagates TLE #1 forward to TLE #2's epoch, then computes how far off the predicted altitude is from what Space-Track actually observed. A residual under ~1km means your drag/SRP model is working; larger systematic residuals point to attitude or atmospheric density modeling errors. This is what you'll write about in section 6.6 of your white paper.
Pass Prediction — takes your TinyGS ground station coordinates and computes every pass in the next 72 hours, with max elevation, AOS/LOS times, and a quality rating. Durham NC is pre-loaded as the default.
Orbital Elements — shows the full Keplerian state for every TLE you've ingested, plus a BSTAR trend chart. Rising BSTAR = increasing drag = your sail is descending faster, which is either winning the race or losing depending on your strategy.
Workflow: Click "Load sample" in the sidebar to see it all working immediately with 4 synthetic TLEs, then replace those with real Space-Track data as you receive it. Use Export JSON to persist your TLE history across sessions.
